# Article blueprint

## Shared subject

The default shared subject is:

The legal digital personality: from a 2019 robots approach to ai in 2026

## Working title pattern

Use a title of this form when it fits:

### English
From Robots to Legal Digital Personality: [specific subtitle about law, taxation, or social insurance], 2019-2026

### French
De la personnalite juridique des robots a celle de l'intelligence artificielle: [sous-titre sur le droit, la fiscalite ou les assurances sociales], 2019-2026

## Contribution pattern

A strong BSCECOM18 article usually contributes by doing three things:
1. recovering the original thesis and its logic
2. showing why the 2019 robot framing is incomplete in 2026 for ai legal personality questions
3. replacing the narrow robot-status lens with a broader and more defensible ai-era legal and fiscal architecture

## Recommended 20-page architecture

### 1. Introduction
- motivate the topic
- state the research question
- state the contribution
- preview the argument

### 2. Literature review
- robot legal personality
- ai legal personality and governance
- automation and labor share
- social insurance financing
- tax and liability implications

### 3. Evolution from 2019 to 2026
- why robots were central in 2019
- why ai becomes central by 2026
- what changed analytically and legally

### 4. Conceptual framework
- define robots, automation, software, and ai systems
- define legal personality precisely
- explain the mechanism that links technology to legal status, taxation, and public finance

### 5. Legal analysis
- compare legal-category options
- discuss feasibility and doctrinal constraints
- identify the strongest architecture

### 6. Fiscal and macroeconomic implications
- taxable-subject design
- labor share
- productivity
- transition costs
- inequality and social insurance financing

### 7. Proposed extension model
- present the paper's new framework
- justify it against alternatives
- explain expected implications

### 8. Discussion
- limits
- counterarguments
- policy boundaries

### 9. Conclusion
- answer the question directly
- state what remains uncertain
- suggest future research

## Tone requirements
- rigorous
- explicit about assumptions
- modest about claims
- interdisciplinary without becoming vague
- suitable for parallel english and french articles built from the same expert base
