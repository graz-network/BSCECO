# Workflow

## Default execution order

1. Intake and source map
2. Macro economist review
3. Fiscality review
4. Law doctor review
5. Researcher synthesis english
6. Researcher synthesis french
7. Originality and extension planning
8. Article blueprint
9. Final english researcher-written latex draft
10. Final french researcher-written latex draft
11. Optional pdf rendering

## Stage 1: Intake and source map

Capture:
- the thesis title and topic
- the original research question
- the original result
- the bibliography clusters
- the research gap between the thesis year and the target year
- the shared subject lock

For BSCECOM18, the default gap is 2019 to 2026 and the central thematic question is:
- "the legal digital personality: from a 2019 robots approach to ai in 2026"

## Stage 2: Expert reviews

Keep the expert outputs separate. Do not merge them too early.

Each expert should produce:
- retained insights
- obsolete assumptions
- updated framing
- open problems
- recommended extension angle

## Stage 3: Researcher syntheses

Each researcher agent synthesizes the expert outputs independently.

The english and french syntheses:
- share the same research subject
- may differ in emphasis, framing, and argumentative sequence
- must remain traceable to the same expert base
- must not be reduced to translation pairs

## Stage 4: Final writing

Each researcher agent writes the article only after deciding independently:
- article title
- contribution
- section order
- normative scope
- argumentative emphasis

## Documentation rule

For every stage, document:
- input used
- transformation performed
- output produced
- unresolved questions
